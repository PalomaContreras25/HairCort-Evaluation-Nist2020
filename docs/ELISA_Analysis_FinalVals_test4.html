Accuracy (using mean for A + spike contribution)

Accuracy, or recovery %, is the ratio between observed and expected concentration, multiplied by 100. Expected values are defined using the dilution factor, starting with the concentration value of the neat sample.

  • 80–120% is generally acceptable for biological assays.

  • 70–130% may be acceptable at very low concentrations.
